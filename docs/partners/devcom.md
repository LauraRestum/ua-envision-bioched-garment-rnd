# DEVCOM / Government

## Role in This Model

DEVCOM and related defense entities fund and evaluate new textile and soldier system innovations. In this model, the government is the end customer and the primary funding source.

They do not develop materials or build garments. They define requirements, allocate funding, and evaluate whether a system meets those requirements.

## Evaluation Priorities

- Speed to prototype and test
- Functional performance against defined requirements
- Scalable manufacturing pathway (can it move beyond prototype?)
- Risk reduction (is the team credible? is the technology proven in adjacent contexts?)

## Primary Funding Pathway

**OTA (Other Transaction Authority)**

OTA is the preferred entry point for this concept. It is designed for rapid prototyping, moves faster than traditional procurement, and does not require a final product — only a defined prototype and a capable team.

Key requirement: a specific, defined use case. Generic pitches are not funded.

## Supporting Pathways

| Pathway | Notes |
|---|---|
| BAA (DEVCOM, ARL) | Broader R&D funding; longer timeline |
| SBIR/STTR | Small business focused; can support Envision-led efforts |
| AFFOA | Advanced Functional Fabrics of America; textile-specific mandate |

## What This Partnership Offers the Government

- A defined use case: bio/chem protective garment
- A credible team with material innovation + manufacturing capability
- A fast path to prototype and evaluation
- Reduced integration risk — materials, prototyping, and manufacturing are pre-coordinated
