# Briefing Structure

## Format

A controlled, linear interactive briefing. One section at a time. No navigation. The audience moves through it in sequence.

Designed for a live meeting with Under Armour and defense R&D stakeholders.

## Tone

- Direct
- Technical but digestible
- No filler, no marketing language
- Every section should answer: "so what does this mean in practice?"

See [Constraints](constraints.md) for full do/don't rules.

---

## Sections

### 1. Opening — The System

Introduce the three-party model without over-explaining any individual company.

Goal: establish that this is a structured system, not a vague collaboration. Each party has a distinct role.

> UA develops materials. Envision builds the garment systems. The government funds and evaluates. That's the model.

---

### 2. Problem — The Bottleneck

State the problem clearly and specifically.

Goal: make it obvious that the current state is broken, and that the problem is structural — not a lack of technology.

> Bio/chem protection requires multi-layer systems. Current development pipelines can't iterate fast enough. Material innovation is ahead of the manufacturing and integration capacity to deploy it.

---

### 3. Model — Integrated Roles

Show how the three parties fit together mechanically.

Goal: make the handoffs clear. UA → Envision → Government is not just a flow chart — it's a defined sequence where each party removes the friction the previous one creates.

---

### 4. Use Case — Bio/Chem Garment

Anchor the concept to a specific application.

Goal: make this concrete. Not "protective garments" in the abstract — a specific system solving a specific tradeoff problem.

> [Garment concept to be defined — see Open Questions]

---

### 5. Pathway — OTA → Prototype → Evaluation → Scale

Walk through the execution sequence.

Goal: show that this is fundable and executable, not theoretical. The pathway exists. OTA is the entry point. Prototype is the deliverable. Evaluation leads to scale.

---

### 6. Why It Works — Speed, Integration, Risk Reduction

Address the "why this team" question directly.

Goal: make the case that this partnership is structurally superior to alternatives — not because of brand names, but because of capability fit and speed.

- Speed: Envision compresses prototype timelines
- Integration: materials and manufacturing are pre-coordinated
- Risk reduction: the government isn't coordinating three separate vendors

---

### 7. Next Step — Define Pilot + Funding Path

Close with a specific ask.

Goal: end the briefing with a decision, not a feeling. What needs to be agreed on to move forward?

> Define the pilot garment concept. Identify the OTA sponsor. Set a prototype timeline.
