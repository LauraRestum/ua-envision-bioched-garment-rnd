# Funding Landscape

## Primary Pathway: OTA

**Other Transaction Authority** is the recommended first entry point.

- Designed for rapid prototyping — not final procurement
- Moves significantly faster than traditional FAR-based contracts
- Does not require a fully developed product; requires a defined prototype and a capable team
- DEVCOM and related entities actively use OTA for textile and soldier system innovation

**What makes this OTA-ready:**
- Defined use case (bio/chem protective garment)
- Credible team with complementary, non-overlapping capabilities
- Clear prototype pathway via Envision's rapid manufacturing capability
- Material foundation from UA's existing R&D

**Critical requirement:** The pitch must be specific. A vague capability statement will not be funded. The use case, the team structure, and the prototype plan must be concrete.

## Supporting Pathways

### BAA — Broad Agency Announcement (DEVCOM, ARL)
- Broader scope R&D funding
- Longer timeline than OTA
- Relevant if the concept needs earlier-stage validation before a prototype commitment

### SBIR / STTR
- Small business focused
- Can support Envision-led efforts if structured correctly
- STTR requires a research institution partner; SBIR does not

### AFFOA — Advanced Functional Fabrics of America
- Textile-specific mandate aligned directly with this use case
- Government-industry consortium; membership or partnership may be required
- Worth pursuing in parallel as a validation and credibility pathway

## Funding Exists — The Gap is Specificity

None of these pathways are speculative. The funding mechanisms are active. The barrier is not availability — it is presenting a use case specific enough to trigger sponsor alignment.

That is what this briefing is designed to do.
